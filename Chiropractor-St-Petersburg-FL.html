Chiropractic has come a long way ...


In 1895, Daniel David Palmer, the dad of modern-day chiropractic, performed the first chiropractic adjustment on a patient of his who had actually lost his hearing after a back injury some 17 years previously. The patient reported that after Dr. Palmer adjusted a vertebra in his upper back, some of his hearing returned.


In its infancy, chiropractic was considered part approach, part art, and part science. Although the practice had actually been around for centuries, no one-- before Palmer, that is-- had drawn connections in between the how and why of spine control.


For the next 120+ years, chiropractic care has grown and developed into what it is today: an around the world, acknowledged treatment for all types of skeletal discomfort and conditions, and an option approach of treatment for many other conditions.
